# Product Identity

Time Tracker is not primarily an AI service. Its job is to help a person create clean, rich, durable personal data that their own AI can understand, trust, reuse, and act on.

## Decision Principle

Prefer data quality over features that only look smart.

Prioritize:

- clear structure over flashy AI-like behavior
- reliable capture over premature automation
- exportability and reuse over app-local lock-in
- user-owned context over provider-owned inference
- consistency over short-term novelty

## Anti-Shallow Checklist

Before accepting a feature, UI change, or shortcut, ask:

1. Does it help the user generate better data for their own AI?
2. Does it preserve meaning, context, timestamps, and intent in reusable form?
3. Does it reduce ambiguity instead of merely feeling smarter?
4. Would it still matter if built-in AI disappeared?
5. Does it help the user think, record, and hand off better instead of replacing that with shallow convenience?

Weak answers mean the decision is drifting from the product identity.
